Find Things To Do

Sunday,

August 06,

9 a.m.

Walk it Off

Bellevue State Park

Picked up a few extra pounds? Or just want to get outside and enjoy the park? Start your week off right with our Monday morning walks. We’ll provide information and charts to track your progress. There is no cost or pre-registration necessary. May be cancelled due to inclement weather. Meet at the track.

Wednesday,

August 1,

10 a.m.

Barnes Woods Winter Hike

Trap Pond State Park

Come hiking in a winter wonderland. bring warm clothes and comfortable shoes. Trip will state at Trap Pond Nature Center and we will drive to Barnes Woods for a 45-minute hike. Total program time will be approximately 2-1/2 hours. Call (302) 875-5163 by February 12 to register. Free

Friday,

August 4,

10 a.m.

Tales of Slavery and Freedom Walking Tour

First State Heritage Park

As a border state, Delaware held a complicated position regarding slavery. Tours led by historical interpreters in period dress begin at the John Bell House on The Green every hour and last approximately 45 minutes.

Saturday,

August 5,

9 a.m.

Park Hike

Cape Henlopen State Park

Picked up a few extra pounds? Or just want to get outside and enjoy the park? Start your week off right with our Monday morning walks. We’ll provide information and charts to track your progress. There is no cost or pre-registration necessary. May be cancelled due to inclement weather. Meet at the track.

Monday,

August 6,

10 a.m.

Owl Prowl

Brandywine Creek State Park

Look for owls. In trees. Or barns or something

Thursday,

August 3,

9 a.m.

Extreme Sports Camp

Bellevue State Park

Travel to different sites each day for exciting activities such as paintballing, parasailing, rock climbing, archery and more! Safety and team building are also important parts to this awesome camp. No experience is necessary. Call for an updated list of activities for each week. Trips involve travel to sites 15 minutes to 1.5 hours away.$330

Thursday,

August 3,

8 P.m.

Ghost Crab Walk

Cape Henlopen State Park

Walk the bay in search of these elusive crustaceans and discover why they come out at night. Bring a flashlight to help spot them and dress for the weather. For ages 7 and up with a paying adult.Limit 20.Pre-registration required by calling (302) 645-6852.$5 per person

Thursday,

August 3,

12:15 P.m.

Heavy Artillery Demonstration

Fort Delaware State Park

BOOM! Fort Delaware has one of the largest Civil War-era cannons that still fires on a regular basis. Don't miss this explosive program that is regularly our most popular daily event!

Thursday,

August 3,

3 p.m.

Jr. Nature Rangers

Cape Henlopen State Park

Investigate the wildlife and nature of Cape Henlopen through hands-on games, crafts and exploring. Each week offers a new topic for children ages 6 to 9. Limit 12 children.Pre-registration required by calling (302) 645-6852.$3 per child

Thursday,

August 3,

9:30 a.m.

Kayak Eco-Tour: Exploring "Station Cove"

Delaware Seashore State Park

Paddle a sit-on-top kayak through the marshes of Rehoboth Bay and catch a unique glimpse of the historic Indian River Life-Saving Station. Along this 2.5 mile journey, park naturalists will identify the wildlife that makes up this dynamic ecosystem. Participants must wear closed-toe shoes or will not be able to come. Remember to bring sunscreen and a water bottle. Pre-registration required. Ages 13 and up; under 16 must be accompanied by a paying adult. $35 per person.Please call (302)-227-6991 to register.

Thursday,

August 3,

2:15 p.m.

Musketry Demonstration

Fort Delaware State Park

The weapons of the Civil War were much more effective than in America's previous wars. Come to this program if you want to know why (or if you just like the smell of gunpowder)!

Thursday,

August 3,

2:15 p.m.

Nature on the Go

Alapocas Run/Wilmington State Parks

Take to the sky with our naturalists as we discover the world of astronomy in our parks. Drop in each week to discover more about a different part of our solar system and galaxy.

Thursday,

August 3,

8 p.m.

Night Hike: Indian River Inlet Bridge

Delaware Seashore State Park

Hike with a naturalist over the Charles W. Cullen bridge and experience it in all its glory when it's illuminated at night! Learn about the history of the inlet and the bridge as you stroll to the top of this iconic structure. Meet at South Inlet Day Parking area. Pre-registration required. $6 per person.Please call (302)-227-6991 to register.

Thursday,

August 3,

11:15 a.m.

Pea Patch History Tour

Fort Delaware State Park

Did you know that there were other forts on the same location before Fort Delaware? Come learn about them and how Fort Delaware was developed with our Pea Patch History Tour.

Thursday,

August 3,

1:15 p.m.

Quarters Tour

Fort Delaware State Park

Visit the home of Captain and Mrs. John Young. See how an officer lives at Fort Delaware, and hear about what it is like to work for one of the fort's wealthy citizens.

Thursday,

August 3,

9 a.m.

Recreation Camp: Moovin and Groovin

Bellevue State Park

Ages 7-11 Bellevue State Park is the place to be this summer! Activities include sports and games, nature exploration, arts and crafts, and a weekly field trip outside of the park all related to the weekly theme. $185 per week

Thursday,

August 3,

9 a.m.

Seining the Bay

Cape Henlopen State Park

Use a beach seine net to search the shallows of Delaware Bay for fish, crabs and other creatures. Dress to get wet and wear secure shoes, no flip flops!For ages 5 and older with a paying adult.No reservations needed. Tickets are available the day of the program with a maximum of six tickets per group. Limit 30. $5 per person

Thursday,

August 3,

9 a.m.

Squid Dissection: From Pen to Ink!

Delaware Seashore State Park

Use a beach seine net to search the shallows of Delaware Bay for fish, crabs and other creatures. Dress to get wet and wear secure shoes, no flip flops!For ages 5 and older with a paying adult.No reservations needed. Tickets are available the day of the program with a maximum of six tickets per group. Limit 30. $5 per person
